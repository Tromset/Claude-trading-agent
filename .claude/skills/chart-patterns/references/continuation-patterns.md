# Continuation Patterns — Detailed Reference

Continuation patterns form mid-trend as a pause/consolidation before the trend resumes. They resolve in the direction of the prior trend.

## Bull Flag

**Formation:** Strong impulsive move up (the "pole"), followed by a shallow downward-sloping channel (the "flag").

**Criteria:**
- Prior impulsive move of at least 5% in < 10 bars (the pole).
- Flag retraces 30-50% of the pole (shallow; deep flags are suspect).
- Flag takes 5-15 bars to form (tight; extended flags lose energy).
- Volume contracts during the flag; expands on the breakout.
- Confirmed on close above the upper flag boundary.

**Target:** Length of the pole projected from the breakout point.

**Stop:** Below the lowest point of the flag.

**R:R typical:** 2:1 to 3:1.

**Reliability:** ~70%. Higher on shorter timeframes (1H, 4H) where the momentum is fresh.

## Bear Flag

Mirror of bull flag. Sharp drop (pole), then shallow upward-sloping consolidation (flag).

**Criteria:** Same as bull flag, inverted. Breakout below the lower flag boundary.

**Reliability:** ~68%. Slightly lower because declines tend to be sharper and less orderly.

## Pennant (bull / bear)

**Formation:** Like a flag but the consolidation is a small symmetrical triangle rather than a parallel channel.

**Criteria:**
- Prior impulsive move (pole).
- Converging trendlines forming a small triangle (< 15 bars).
- Volume declines into the apex.
- Breakout in the direction of the prior move.

**Target:** Pole length projected from breakout.

**Reliability:** ~65%. Slightly less reliable than flags because the symmetrical triangle can break either way.

## Symmetrical Triangle

**Formation:** Converging trendlines (lower highs + higher lows) creating a narrowing range.

**Criteria:**
- At least 4 touches of the trendlines (2 upper, 2 lower).
- Breakout typically occurs between 50-75% of the way to the apex.
- Volume should contract as the triangle forms.
- Breakout direction matches the prior trend ~60% of the time.

**Target:** Height of the triangle (base, measured at the widest point) projected from the breakout.

**Stop:** Beyond the opposite trendline.

**Reliability:** ~55% for continuation; can go either way. Use with trend bias from higher timeframe.

## Ascending Triangle (bullish bias)

**Formation:** Flat upper resistance + rising lower trendline (higher lows).

**Criteria:**
- At least 2 touches of the flat resistance, 2 touches of the rising trendline.
- Volume declines into the apex.
- Breakout above resistance with volume.

**Target:** Height of triangle (from flat resistance to lowest low) projected upward.

**Stop:** Below the most recent higher low.

**Reliability:** ~70% bullish. One of the highest-reliability continuation patterns in uptrends.

## Descending Triangle (bearish bias)

Mirror: flat lower support + falling upper trendline (lower highs).

**Criteria:** Same as ascending, inverted. Breakout below support.

**Reliability:** ~68% bearish.

## Rectangle (range / box)

**Formation:** Horizontal support and resistance containing price for an extended period.

**Criteria:**
- At least 2 touches of both support and resistance.
- Clear, well-defined boundaries (not sloppy).
- Duration: typically 10-40 bars.
- Volume may decline inside; expands on breakout.
- Direction: typically resolves in the prior trend direction, but can go either way.

**Target:** Height of the rectangle projected from breakout.

**Stop:** Opposite boundary of the rectangle.

**Reliability:** ~60% for continuation direction. More reliable when prior trend is strong.

## Cup and Handle (bullish)

**Formation:** U-shaped correction (the "cup") followed by a small flag/drift lower (the "handle"), then breakout above the cup's rim.

**Criteria:**
- Cup is rounded (not V-shaped) and typically 7-65 bars.
- Depth of cup: 12-33% correction is ideal (O'Neil specification).
- Handle retraces < 50% of the cup's depth and slopes slightly downward.
- Handle volume is light; breakout volume should be > 1.5× average.
- Confirmed on close above the cup rim (the prior high).

**Target:** Depth of the cup projected upward from the rim.

**Stop:** Below the handle low.

**Reliability:** ~70%. Made famous by William O'Neil / CANSLIM methodology.

## Rising Wedge (bearish despite rising price)

**Formation:** Price makes higher highs and higher lows, but the trendlines converge upward. Each push up is weaker.

**Criteria:**
- Converging rising trendlines (upper trendline flatter than lower).
- At least 3 touches per line.
- Volume declining as the wedge progresses.
- Breakdown below the lower trendline.

**Target:** Height of wedge at widest point, projected downward.

**Reliability:** ~65% as a reversal. Can also be a continuation in a downtrend.

## Falling Wedge (bullish despite falling price)

Mirror: converging falling trendlines. Breakout upward.

**Reliability:** ~68% as a bullish reversal or downtrend continuation pause.

## Key rules for all continuation patterns

1. **Context is king.** A flag in an uptrend is bullish. The same shape in a downtrend is ambiguous.
2. **Volume contraction during formation, expansion on breakout.** If volume doesn't expand on breakout → higher false-break risk.
3. **Don't anticipate.** Wait for the breakout close, not the first wick beyond the boundary.
4. **Failed continuation patterns can become reversals.** A bull flag that breaks down is a powerful sell signal — stronger than the original flag was a buy signal.
5. **Measured targets are minimums.** Use them for partial profit; trail the rest with a structural stop.
6. **Time limit.** Patterns that take too long to resolve (> 2× their expected duration) lose potency. A bull flag that takes 40 bars is probably not a flag anymore.
