# Reversal Patterns — Detailed Reference

Reversal patterns signal a probable change in the prevailing trend direction. They form at the end of a trend and resolve in the opposite direction.

## Head and Shoulders (bearish reversal)

**Formation:** Three peaks — left shoulder, head (higher), right shoulder (roughly equal to left). Connected by a neckline drawn through the two troughs.

**Criteria:**
- Prior uptrend must exist (at least 20 bars of higher highs).
- Head is the highest peak; shoulders are roughly symmetrical.
- Neckline can slope slightly; steep neckline slopes reduce reliability.
- Volume typically: highest on left shoulder, lower on head, lowest on right shoulder.
- Confirmed on a close below the neckline with volume > 1.2× 20-day average.

**Target:** Measured move = distance from head to neckline, projected downward from neckline break.

**Stop:** Above the right shoulder.

**Reliability:** ~75% when volume confirms. False breaks (close back above neckline within 3 bars) occur ~15% of the time.

**Failure mode:** Rising neckline with strong volume on right shoulder → trend continuation, not reversal.

## Inverse Head and Shoulders (bullish reversal)

Mirror of H&S. Three troughs — left shoulder, head (lowest), right shoulder. Neckline through the two peaks.

**Criteria:** Prior downtrend, head lowest, volume increases on breakout above neckline.

**Target:** Distance from head to neckline, projected upward.

**Stop:** Below the right shoulder.

**Reliability:** ~75%. Slightly higher win rate than bearish H&S due to buying pressure asymmetry.

## Double Top (bearish reversal)

**Formation:** Two peaks at approximately the same level (within 1-3%), separated by a trough.

**Criteria:**
- Prior uptrend.
- Peaks within 3% of each other.
- Trough between peaks at least 10% below the peaks (otherwise it's noise).
- Volume often lower on second peak.
- Confirmed on a close below the trough (support) level.

**Target:** Height of pattern (peak to trough) projected downward from the break.

**Stop:** Above the higher of the two peaks + 1× ATR(14).

**Reliability:** ~70%. Higher if volume diverges (lower on second peak).

**Common trap:** The "double-top" that breaks down 1% then rips to a new high. Wait for a decisive close below support, not just a wick.

## Double Bottom (bullish reversal)

Mirror of double top. Two troughs at approximately the same level, separated by a peak.

**Criteria:** Prior downtrend, troughs within 3%, confirmed above the peak between them.

**Target:** Height of pattern projected upward.

**Stop:** Below the lower trough.

**Reliability:** ~72%. The "W" shape is one of the most recognizable and reliable reversal patterns.

## Triple Top / Triple Bottom

Three tests of the same level, each failing to break through. Rarer than double, but higher reliability when confirmed (~78%).

**Entry:** On break below the support zone (triple top) or above resistance (triple bottom).

**Target:** Height of pattern projected from break.

## Rounded Bottom (saucer)

Gradual shift from selling to buying pressure. Very slow forming (weeks to months).

**Criteria:**
- Extended base (at least 30 bars on daily).
- Volume U-shaped (heavy early, light at bottom, increasing on rise).
- Breakout above the left-side high.

**Target:** Depth of the saucer projected upward. Often opens a long-term uptrend.

**Stop:** Below the deepest point of the saucer.

**Reliability:** High (~80%) but takes patience. Not suitable for day or fast-swing trading.

## V-Reversal (spike reversal)

Sharp, sudden reversal with no base. Driven by capitulation or euphoria.

**Criteria:**
- Extreme move (> 3× ATR in one session) followed by an equally sharp reversal.
- Volume climax on the reversal bar (highest bar in weeks).
- Often at major support/resistance or news events.

**Target:** Typically retraces 50-100% of the spike.

**Stop:** Beyond the spike extreme + 0.5× ATR.

**Reliability:** ~60%. Low reliability because there's no base — but when it works, the move is fast and large.

**Warning:** V-reversals are hard to trade in real-time. Most of the reversal happens in a single bar. Prefer double/triple bottoms for safer entries.

## Key rules for all reversal patterns

1. **Requires a prior trend.** A reversal pattern in a range is meaningless.
2. **Volume confirms.** Breakout with volume > 1.5× average is far more reliable.
3. **Wait for the close.** Intrabar breaks frequently fail. Confirmation = close beyond the breakout level.
4. **Retest is normal.** ~60% of valid breakouts retest the breakout level before continuing. Plan entries accordingly.
5. **Measured move is a minimum target,** not a guarantee. Take partial profit at 1× measured move, trail the rest.
6. **Stops go at the pattern's invalidation level,** not an arbitrary distance. For H&S: above right shoulder. For double bottom: below the lower low.
